About Us

A family in Duck Run 2.

We've grown slowly — at the speed of the Holy Spirit and friendship. This is our story so far.

Who We Are

We're a Pentecostal Christian congregation in Duck Run 2, a village of around 455 residents in Cayo District, just west of Spanish Lookout.

The church is small but active: families of every age — children, youth, and grandparents — who gather twice a week to worship, study the Bible, and pray. A children's Bible school is currently under construction on the church grounds.

Our Denomination

We belong to Full Gospel Church of God Belize (FGCOG), a Pentecostal denomination with a national presence — Belmopan, Santa Elena, Orange Walk, San Pedro, San José, Santa Familia, and more.

With the national body we share a clear emphasis: the centrality of Christ, the authority of the Bible, the work of the Holy Spirit, and evangelism as a daily mission.

What We Believe

We believe in the verbal inspiration of the Bible and in one God who exists eternally in three persons: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit. We believe that Jesus Christ is the only-begotten of the Father, conceived of the Holy Spirit, born of the virgin Mary; that He was crucified, buried, rose from the dead, ascended to heaven, and is today at the right hand of the Father as our Intercessor.

We believe that all have sinned and that repentance is necessary for the forgiveness of sins; that justification, regeneration, and the new birth are brought about by faith in the blood of Jesus Christ; and that holiness is God's standard of life for His people. We believe in water baptism by immersion, in the baptism of the Holy Spirit with the initial evidence of speaking in tongues, in divine healing provided in the atonement, and in the second coming of Jesus — with the bodily resurrection, eternal life for the righteous, and eternal punishment for the wicked.

Vision:
Healthy churches that multiply, with highly prepared, creative, innovative, and committed leaders.
Mission:
To equip, empower, and facilitate the work of the local church for the fulfillment of the Great Commission.
